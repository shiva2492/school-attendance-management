Only For Full Time Staff
Dear staff member,
As from July 25, a new ‘Leave System’ will be introduced at Math Vision to ensure a smoother functioning for the management and the staff. The number of leaves allocated has been revised to 24, and the new breakdown is as follows:
  • Sick Leaves: 14
  • Annual Leaves: 10
The 24 leaves will be equally distributed over 12 months, whereby each staff will be allowed 2 days of leave every month. Note that leaves will not be carried forward on a monthly basis.
The following scenarios will provide a clearer picture of the new ‘Leave System’:
a) Scenario 1.
Assuming a monthly salary of $3000, if an employee has not taken any leave in a particular month (25th July to 24th August). The salary for the month of August (Aug 1 - Aug 30) will be calculated as follows:
Employee was entitled to 2 days of leave. Since no single leave was taken for that month, the employee will be paid for an extra 2 days which equals:
(3000/26) x 2 = 230.76
The total salary for this month will be: $3000 + $230.76 = $3230.76.
b) Scenario 2.
Assuming a monthly salary of $3000, if an employee has taken 3 days leave in a particular month (25th July to 24th August). The salary for the month of August (Aug 1 - Aug 30) will be calculated as follows:
Employee was entitled to 2 days of leave but exceeded leaves allowed for the month by 1 day. The resulting deduction for August equals:
(3000/26) x 1 = 115.38
The total salary for this month will be: $3000 - $115.38 = $2884.62.
c) Scenario 3.
Assuming a monthly salary of $3000, if an employee has taken 2 days leave in a particular month (25th July to 24th August). The salary for the month of August (Aug 1 - Aug 30) will be calculated as follows:
Employee was entitled to 2 days of leave, equal to the number of leaves taken for the month.
Hence, salary paid for the month will be equal to the actual salary of $3000 for the month of November.
d) Scenario 4.
Assuming a monthly salary of $3000, if an employee has taken 10 days leave in a particular month (25th July to 24th August). The salary for the month of August (Aug 1 - Aug 30) will be calculated as follows:
Employee was entitled to 2 days of leave but exceeded leaves allowed for the month by 8 days. The resulting deduction for November equals:
(3000/26) x 8 = 923.07
The total salary for this month will be: $3000 - $923.07 = $2076.93.
Important Note: The above calculation will also apply in the case of long leaves. Hence, you are requested to plan for your long leaves financially.
Only For Full Time Staff
Dear All,
We wish to inform you about a change in the company's medical Insurance plan. Up to now, we have been using AXA's 'co-pay $20' plan. This plan proved beneficial by lightening the financial burden faced by our staff due to medical bills.
Unfortunately, it also proved limited in terms of coverage and benefits on some occasions, particularly in the case of hospitalisation. Medical cost in Singapore is one of the highest and we feel that it is our duty to protect our staff members against unexpected medical bills. When care is needed, we want to focus on recovering rather than stressing over medical bills.
Hence, We have decided to change the company's insurance plan to one which boasts a higher level of benefits and coverage to further help staff members to meet the expenses in case of medical emergencies. We are still finalising the details of the new policy and further details will be communicated to you soon.
Note that, in the meantime, the old plan will not be valid. It is a small inconvenience that will surely be outweighed by the benefits of the new plan. We will inform you further very soon.
Divesh Shah

Only For Full Time Staff
Dear teachers,
Please be informed that Math Vision has decided to revamp its performance appraisal system which has been serving as basis for deciding upon promotions and salary increments.
A more individualized system that gives more prominence to personal performances will come into effect as from January 01, 2017. The hallmarks of the new system will be:
  • Attendance – based on how regularly teacher takes leaves.
  • Punctuality – based on the number of lateness per month.
  • Team-Spirit – willingness to help and train colleagues, and co-operate with in-charges.
  • Versatility – ability and willingness to teach different subjects, grades and syllabuses.
  • Sense of responsibility – Based on how often teacher takes leaves on weekend and length of notice period for long-term leaves.
Hence, more emphasis will be laid upon the talent of teachers, their attitude towards work and their willingness to learn and progress within the company. The new system will be jointly managed by the in-charges and me. As such, in-charges will be given the responsibility to individually rate and evaluate the performances of teachers according to the criteria listed above. These internal feedbacks will be the predominant factor affecting chances of promotion or salary increments.
Please inform me in case you need any clarification with respect to the new performance appraisal system.
Divesh Shah
Math Vision.
